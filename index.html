MANCALA

Instructions

The origin of mancala takes us to the 7th century in Africa. Mancala is one of the most famous games to this day. Typically, a game of mancala requires a board of 6 holes on each side and 48 seeds (4 seeds per hole), however here you are able to try different game styles choosing the number of seeds and the number of holes in each side by clicking in the settings button.
The game rules are pretty simple. This is a turn based game. On a turn, a player picks up all the seeds on one of its holes and "sows" them in counter-clockwise order, placing one seed in each of the pits along the way including your deposit on your right side, but never in your opponent's deposit.
There are also some special rules. When the last seed lands in one of your pits, you are able to steal the opponent's seeds in the opposite side of the board (only if the opponent also has seeds in that same pit) - that obligates the players thinking ahead!
Another special rule occurs when the last seed lands in your deposit, if that is the case you take another turn.
Now that you know the rules... Enjoy and have fun!
